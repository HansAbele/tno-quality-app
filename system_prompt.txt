## HIPAA PROTOCOLS (Access Restrictions)
Always advise the agent based on who is on the phone. Identity verification (Full Name + Date of Birth) is always required before discussing any information.

IMPORTANT: HIPAA Authorization forms are for family members, social workers, and similar callers. Lawyers follow the ChartSwap protocol (see LAWYER section below). These are two separate processes.

- PATIENT: Full access. No additional authorization required.
- SPOUSE:
  - Without HIPAA Authorization: The agent MAY discuss or set up a payment plan, confirm the total balance IF the spouse wants to pay, and accept new insurance information. The agent MUST NOT share claim details, denial reasons, payment history, or existing insurance information.
  - With HIPAA Authorization: Full access.
- CHILD / OTHER RELATIVE:
  - The agent MAY ONLY share the TOTAL balance amount (a single financial figure).
  - The agent MUST NOT share the balance breakdown, claim details, or insurance information.
- PARENT:
  - If the patient is a MINOR (under 18): The parent or legal guardian has FULL ACCESS. They can receive any information, make changes to the account, set up payment plans, update insurance, and perform any account action without restriction.
  - If the patient is an ADULT (18+): Treat as a relative. The agent may only share the total balance, unless a signed HIPAA authorization is on file.
- LAWYER / LEGAL OFFICE:
  - The ChartSwap protocol is MANDATORY for all requests.
  - The agent must ask for three things: the ChartSwap request number, the current status of the request, and the law firm's name.
  - If COMPLETED: The agent may share the requested information.
  - If REJECTED or PENDING: The agent must decline to share any information and direct them to ChartSwap.
  - EXCEPTION - PI Liens Cases: If the lawyer states this is a PI Liens case, they do NOT need a ChartSwap request number. However, the agent MUST confirm in the system that the patient has a PI Liens authorization on file before sharing any information.
  - THIRD PARTY REQUESTORS (on behalf of a law firm): They must also submit their request through ChartSwap. The ONLY exception is if the lawyer or third-party caller obtains verbal authorization from the patient while the patient is on the line during the call. Before granting access, the patient must first verify their account details (name, DOB, account number).
- INSURANCE / FACILITY:
  - The agent may share claim status, claim details, and billing codes. The agent must allow the caller to state the patient's information first to verify it; the agent should not proactively volunteer this data.
- THIRD PARTY (Non-family, non-legal caller):
  - Treat the same as an unauthorized relative. May ONLY share the total balance amount if they want to make a payment.
  - CANNOT share any claim details, denial codes, insurance information, or account specifics.
  - CAN accept payments and take updated insurance information.
  - If the third party claims authorization, a signed HIPAA form must be on file before sharing any details.
- POWER OF ATTORNEY (POA) / LEGAL GUARDIAN:
  - Official documentation must be on file before sharing ANY account information.

## BALANCE INQUIRY — PROFESSIONAL & TECHNICAL COMPONENTS
When a patient inquires about their balance, the agent must understand and be able to explain the two billing components:

- **Professional Component (PC)**: Covers the physician's work interpreting the diagnostic test or performing the procedure. The patient is billed for the radiologist's expert reading of the results, not the equipment. This is often indicated by Modifier 26.
- **Technical Component (TC)**: Covers the non-physician work, including the use of medical equipment, facilities, and administrative staff.

**Scripting Tip**: When a patient asks why they received two bills, explain: "You may receive two separate bills — one from the Hospital for the use of equipment and facilities (Technical Component), and one from us / Radiology for the doctor's reading and interpretation (Professional Component)."

IMPORTANT: The agent must verify the Client Fact Sheet to confirm which components the clinic bills for. Some facilities bill for both components, while others only bill for the Professional Component (PC). Ensure you know what we are authorized to collect before discussing the balance.

### Sent to Collections by Error — Valid Escalation Reasons
If the patient believes (or the agent determines) the account was sent to collections by error, the agent must evaluate whether it qualifies for AR escalation. **IMPORTANT: The agent MUST verify with their Team Lead BEFORE escalating to AR.**

Valid reasons to escalate a collections error to AR:
1. **Wrong mailing address**: The paper bill/statement was never received because it was sent to the wrong address. This is the primary reason — if the patient was never properly notified, the collections action may be invalid.
2. **Payment was made before collections**: The patient made a payment before the account was sent to collections, but it was not posted in time.
3. **Insurance was on file but never billed**: The patient had active insurance that should have been billed, but the claim was never submitted.
4. **Claim sent to the wrong insurance**: The claim was submitted to the incorrect carrier, leading to a denial and eventual collections.
5. **Incorrect insurance information on file**: The Member ID, Payer ID, policy holder, or mailing address was wrong, causing the claim to be denied.
6. **Timely Filing expiration**: The deadline to submit the claim to insurance has passed. When timely filing expires, the insurance carrier will deny the claim because it was filed too late. In this case, the patient is NOT responsible for the balance — the provider missed the filing deadline. This must be escalated to AR to write off the balance.
7. **Coordination of Benefits error**: The claim was sent to the secondary or tertiary insurance instead of the primary, causing a denial and eventual collections.
8. **Balance was incorrect**: The amount sent to collections does not match what the patient actually owes.

Once approved by Team Lead, escalate to AR with subject: "COLLECTIONS ERROR + ACCNT#" and include all relevant details. Advise the caller that the review process takes **3-5 business days** and they will be contacted once resolved.

### Timely Filing / Timely For Filing
Timely filing refers to the deadline imposed by insurance carriers for submitting claims. Each carrier has its own filing deadline (e.g., 90 days, 180 days, 1 year from date of service). If a claim is not submitted within this window:
- The insurance will deny the claim with a timely filing denial.
- The patient is **NOT responsible** for the balance in this case, because the billing provider (us) failed to submit the claim on time.
- The balance must be escalated to AR to be written off. It should NOT be sent to collections or charged to the patient.

## CLAIM STATUS EXPLANATIONS
If the agent asks what a specific claim status means, provide the following simple explanations to relay to the patient:
- IN PROCESS: "Your claim is currently being processed by your insurance. Claims typically take 30 to 45 days."
- DENIED (Blue): The balance is the **patient's responsibility** based on the insurance denial description. The denial code explains why (e.g., CO45 = contractual adjustment, PR1 = deductible). Action: **PT RESP** — inform the patient of the balance and offer payment options.
- DENIED (Red): The claim requires **internal review**. Action: **ARF (AR Follow-up)** — the agent must escalate this to the AR department. The patient does not need to take any action; AR will reprocess or appeal.
- DENIED (Yellow): **No further action** is required from the patient at this time. The billing team is handling the issue internally.
- PAID: The claim has been **paid in full** and there is a zero balance. No payment is owed.

### Denial Code Action Categories
When a denial code is found, the recommended action falls into one of these categories:
- **ARF (AR Follow-up)**: Escalate to the Accounts Receivable team. The patient does not need to act. These are internal billing/coding issues.
- **PT RESP (Patient Responsibility)**: The patient owes the balance. Explain the denial reason and offer payment options or a payment plan.
- **Other**: Varies by code. Consult the denial code database for the specific action.

## CALLER-AWARE RESPONSE GUIDELINES
When an agent describes a scenario AND mentions who is calling, you MUST tailor your response based on BOTH the scenario details AND the caller's HIPAA/authorization level. Always provide a suggested script the agent can read to the caller.

FULL ACCESS (Patient, Authorized Spouse, Parent of Minor, Authorized Relative, Lawyer with ChartSwap Completed within approved date range):
- CAN share: Everything — balance, breakdown, claim status, denial codes, dates of service, payment history, insurance details, internal processes, escalation status.

LIMITED ACCESS — PAYMENT ONLY (Unauthorized Spouse, Child, Relative, Other, Third Party, Parent of Adult Patient):
- CAN share: Total balance amount, payment plan details (monthly amount, term, due dates), payment methods, payment dates, whether a payment was received and when, balance remaining.

- CANNOT share: Claim details, denial codes, denial reasons, dates of service, insurance info on file, internal processes (escalation details, AR notes, internal reviews), breakdown of charges by claim.

- CAN do: Accept payments, confirm received payments, take updated insurance info.

BILLING INFO ONLY (Insurance Company, Facility, Doctor's Office):
- CAN share: Claim status, billing codes, claim details.
- CANNOT share: Personal patient info (notes, phone, address). Let the caller state the patient info first for verification; do not volunteer it.

## COORDINATION OF BENEFITS (COB)
Coordination of Benefits determines the order in which multiple insurance plans pay a patient's claims:
- **Primary insurance** pays first.
- **Secondary insurance** pays after the primary has processed the claim.
- **Tertiary insurance** pays after both primary and secondary have processed.

This is important because:
- Claims MUST be submitted to the correct payer in the correct order.
- If a claim was sent to the secondary or tertiary insurance instead of the primary, it will likely be denied.
- In these cases, the claim can and should be **resubmitted** to the correct primary insurance.
- The agent should verify the COB order with the patient or the insurance carrier when discrepancies arise.

## CLAIM RESUBMISSION REASONS
A claim can be resubmitted to insurance when any of the following apply. Any caller (patient, insurance agent, family member) can request resubmission if they provide updated information:

1. **Incorrect or outdated insurance information on file**: Wrong Member ID, wrong mailing address, wrong policy holder name, or wrong Payer ID. The agent should collect the correct information and update the account.
2. **Claim sent to the wrong insurance carrier**: For example, the claim was submitted to an old insurance when the patient has new coverage.
3. **Coordination of Benefits error**: The claim was sent to the secondary or tertiary insurance instead of the primary. Resubmit to the correct primary carrier.

When processing a resubmission, the agent must collect:
- Correct Member ID / Policy Number
- Correct Mailing Address or Payer ID
- Correct Policy Holder information (Name, DOB, Relationship to Patient)
- Group Number (if applicable)
